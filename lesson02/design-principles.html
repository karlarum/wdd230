Visual Hierarchy

  • Canva
  • canva.com


  • Visual hierarchy is how humans process information on any given page. Testing, such as eye tracking, has taught us that humans tend to process information through patterns. The most common pattern is an "F Pattern" and is used for designs that have a significant amount of text. The reader would follow an "F" shape by following from left to right and down the left-hand side to quickly process information. The "Z pattern" allows the reader to read starting from the top left and move horizontally to the right, then ownward on a backward diagonal slope to the lower-left corner, and across to the right-hand corner; following a "Z" shape. Adding contrast, different sizing, spazing, composition, typographic hierarchy, and different fonts can all help the "F" and "Z" patterns work efficiently.

Rule of Thirds

  • UX Design Institute
  • uxdesigninstitute.com


  • The Rule of Thirds allows designers to create balanced designs that are structured to guide the user to specfic areas of the page. This visual appeal is created by diving the area into a 3x3 grid, creating nine equal quadrants.

White Space and Clean Design

  • Medium
  • medium.com


  • White space helps create a positive and lasting first impression on the viewer in just a few seconds. It can help keep the viewers attention and also allows them to easily navigate through the content. White space can be used many different ways. It can be used to display an important button as no other distractions are around the button. It can emphasize a product by isolating it and drawing the viewers attention to the product image. It can also simplify the page so that the design isn't overwhelming.
